# Writing & Essays

## LLM Hallucination research paper
"The Hallucination Problem in LLMs: Why Scaling Parameters Isn't Enough" - investigates hallucinations in large language models, examining why they occur and how they relate to model size. Uses Gemma-3 models (1B-12B parameters) on the SimpleQA benchmark (4,326 QA pairs). Results: Gemma-1B hallucination rate 97.11%, Gemma-4B 95.19%, Gemma-12B 93.85%. Published in the Curieux Academic Journal.

## Histology classification research paper
"Building the Best Pipeline for Histology Patch Classification" - benchmarks multiple classifier architectures (attention pooling, KNN, MLP) on histology patch embeddings from eight foundation models (Virchow, Virchow2, UNI, UNIv2, Hibou-B, Hibou-L, Phikon, Phikonv2) using the NCTCRCHE100K dataset. Attention pooling on patch tokens consistently achieves best F1 scores.

## Combined research paper (丘竞赛)
"How Changing Neural Network Architectures Improves Performance: Insights from LLM Hallucination, Simple Classification, and Vision Tasks" - a combined paper addressing three research questions: (1) How architecture changes affect LLM hallucination vs parameter scaling, (2) Performance gains in classification tasks, (3) CNNs vs ViTs in visual inspection.

## NYT "Growing up with AI" submission
Submitted a piece to the New York Times "Growing up with AI" contest about the frustration and hope of switching between different AI models. Describes chaotic search histories, memory retention issues between models, and AI as "a necessary step in building the muscle memory for the complex, multi-tool future of work and learning."
